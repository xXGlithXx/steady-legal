2. Data Steady accesses

2.1 Apple HealthKit data (read-only)

With your explicit permission (granted through Apple's own Health permission screen, which Steady cannot bypass or pre-select), Steady reads the following categories from Apple Health:

  • Heart Rate Variability (HRV / SDNN)
  • Resting Heart Rate and Heart Rate (including during workouts)
  • Sleep Analysis
  • Step Count and Distance (walking/running)
  • Flights Climbed, Active Energy, Stand Minutes
  • VO₂ Max, Respiratory Rate, Blood Oxygen, Walking Heart Rate
  • Body Weight and Body Fat Percentage (when available from a connected scale)
  • Workout records (for HR statistics)

Steady never requests write access to HealthKit. It only reads data — it never adds, modifies, or deletes anything in your Apple Health records. You can review or revoke any of these permissions at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Steady.

Apple HealthKit-specific disclosure (required by Apple): Steady will not use or disclose information obtained through HealthKit for advertising or other use-based data mining purposes unrelated to providing health or fitness services. Steady will not sell HealthKit data to advertising platforms, data brokers, or information resellers. Steady will not share HealthKit data with any third party, for any purpose — Steady has no backend server to share it with.

2.2 Information you provide directly

  • Profile info: first name, biological sex, date of birth, height, device type (iPhone / Garmin / Apple Watch) — used to personalize calculations like VO₂ Max and HRV baselines.
  • Username: a local display name stored in the device Keychain. Steady does not verify identity, collect an email or phone number, or require any real-world information to use the app.
  • Workout data: routines, sessions, sets, reps, and weights you log, or import from a Hevy CSV export.
  • Focus session data: categories, session durations, and history you create in the Focus tab.

2.3 Diagnostics (crash logs)

If the app crashes, Steady writes a plain-text diagnostic report (crash type, app/OS version, and a raw stack trace) to a local file on your device. This file is never transmitted automatically. You can view, delete, or manually share a crash log yourself from Settings → About → Diagnostics using the standard iOS share sheet — if you choose to share one (e.g. by email, to help us fix a bug), you decide who it goes to.

2.4 What Steady does not collect

  • No advertising identifiers, no ad SDKs, no advertising of any kind
  • No analytics or usage-tracking SDKs (no Firebase, Mixpanel, Amplitude, etc.)
  • No location data
  • No contacts, photos, microphone, or camera access
  • No cookies (Steady is a native app, not a web view)
  • No third-party integrations that receive your data — Steady previously included an optional Strava connection; it has been fully removed from the app

5. Your rights (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, and similar laws)

Because all of your data is stored exclusively on your own device, most data-subject rights under the EU/UK GDPR, California's CCPA/CPRA, and similar laws are already satisfied by design — there is no copy of your data anywhere else for us to act on. Specifically:

  • Right to access / portability — go to Settings → Data & Privacy → Export Data to generate a JSON export of your workouts and focus sessions at any time.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — use Settings → Data & Privacy → Clear Workouts / Clear Focus Sessions / Full Reset, or simply uninstall the app. Because nothing is stored on a server, deleting the app deletes everything Steady has ever stored about you.
  • Right to rectification — edit your profile at any time in Settings → Health → Profile.
  • Right to restrict / object to processing — revoke HealthKit permissions at any time in iOS Settings → Health → Steady; the app will simply stop reading that category of data.
  • Right to know / delete / opt out of sale (CCPA/CPRA) — Steady does not sell personal information, so there is nothing to opt out of. You may request confirmation of this, or request deletion assistance, by contacting us (see Section 8).

Because Steady has no server-side account system, we generally cannot identify "your" data independently of the device in front of you — if you need help exercising any of these rights beyond what the in-app controls provide, contact us and we will assist to the extent technically possible.

6. Children's privacy

Steady requires users to be at least 14 years old — the date-of-birth picker in onboarding and Settings will not accept a birth date more recent than 14 years before today. Steady does not knowingly collect information from children under 14. If you believe a child under 14 has provided information to Steady, contact us and we will assist with deletion (see Section 3 — since data is local to the device, this is typically resolved by deleting the app).

7. Data retention

Your data is retained on your device for as long as you use the app, until you delete it via the in-app controls described in Section 5, or until you uninstall Steady. Crash logs are retained locally until you manually clear them from Settings → About → Diagnostics.
